Founder's Statement

Alexandro Ghanem

مؤسس حركة حَقّ

"

I started HAQQ because people needed help — and no one was helping.

I didn't grow up in luxury. I grew up in Lebanon. I've seen families lose everything. I've watched mothers beg for food, children study in classrooms with no windows, and elderly people live without medicine or electricity. What hurt the most wasn't just the poverty. It was that no one cared.

That's why I built HAQQ. Not as a project. Not as a movement with a logo and a name. But as a way to get food into hands, roofs back on homes, and dignity back into people's lives.

We're not trying to change the whole world. We're trying to help one family, one school, one street at a time.

HAQQ is about action — not talk.

We give aid.
We deliver food.
We support schools.
We clean streets and rebuild broken homes.
And we do it all with zero hidden agenda.

No party. No fame. No cameras. Just real people helping other real people.

This is not about politics. It's about people who are cold, hungry, and forgotten. That's who I serve. That's why HAQQ exists.

— Alexandro Ghanem

Our Vision

A Better Lebanon

We envision a Lebanon where no child goes hungry, no family is homeless, and no elderly person lacks medicine. A Lebanon restored to its former glory.

No Hunger

A Lebanon where every family has enough food on their table, where children never go to bed hungry, and where nutritious meals are accessible to all.

Safe Shelter

A Lebanon where every person has a safe place to call home, where families aren't displaced, and where communities thrive together in security.

Healthcare Access

A Lebanon where medicine is available to those who need it, where the elderly don't have to choose between food and healthcare, and where medical needs are met.
